## Quality Standards

Every example in this taxonomy meets these criteria:

### ✅ Timeless
- No dated terminology (no "Postmodern," "Feminist," etc.)
- Perennial questions recognizable across centuries
- Will be relevant 500 years hence

### ✅ Complex
- Problems that "doctorates spend lifetimes failing to understand"
- Genuine conceptual difficulty, not mere obscurity
- Multiple layers of depth and sophistication

### ✅ Principle-Based
- Focus on underlying structures, not historical accidents
- Uses figures as lenses, not subjects themselves
- Integrates science as branches of metaphysics

### ✅ Profound
- Touches on ultimate questions (being, truth, goodness, beauty)
- Addresses first principles and final ends
- Relates to the knowledge and love of God

## Example Format

Each detailed example includes:

1. **Precise Formulation**: The exact problem stated clearly
2. **Historical Development**: Ancient → Patristic → Medieval → Modern → Contemporary
3. **Dialectical Structure**: Thesis → Antithesis → Synthesis
4. **Cross-References**: Connections to other categories
5. **Theological Dimension**: Relationship to ultimate concerns
6. **Contemporary Relevance**: Modern manifestations and open problems

## Sample Example

### **I.A. Pure Actuality & First Principles**

**Example 1: The Identity of Pure Actuality with the Unmoved Mover in Aristotelian-Thomistic Metaphysics**

- Aristotle's *Metaphysics* Book Lambda: The necessity of an eternal, unchanging first principle
- The argument from motion: The impossibility of infinite regress in moved movers
- Aquinas's appropriation in *Summa Theologiae* I, Q.2, Art. 3: The First Way
- The distinction between pure act (actus purus) and mixed act-potency (all creatures)
- The ontological foundation: Being as act versus being as potency
- The critique of potentiality as the principle of change and corruption
- The relationship to divine simplicity: No composition of essence and existence

## How to Use This Taxonomy

### For Subject Generation
1. Select a category based on priority (Tier 1: Foundations, Tier 2: Branches, Tier 3: Twigs)
2. Review the detailed examples to understand the level of depth required
3. Use the dialectical structure (thesis-antithesis-synthesis) as template
4. Ensure all quality criteria are met (timeless, complex, principle-based, profound)
5. Cross-reference with related categories

### For Quality Control
1. Check against the four quality criteria
2. Verify proper use of primary sources (Scripture, Fathers, Councils)
3. Ensure appropriate difficulty level (doctoral-level complexity)
4. Confirm timeless terminology (no dated 20th/21st-century jargon)
5. Validate theological orthodoxy (Nicene, Chalcedonian, Palamite)

### For Further Development
