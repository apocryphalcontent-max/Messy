## LXXI: The Quantum Ontologies (Superposition, Entanglement, & Measurement) (75 subjects)

**Research Validation**: Quantum mechanics poses the most profound metaphysical questions since the pre-Socratics. The measurement problem, non-locality, and the nature of superposition challenge classical metaphysics.

**Warranted Scope**: 75 subjects needed for:
- Interpretations of QM (Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Bohm) (30 subjects)
- The measurement problem (20 subjects)
- Quantum non-locality and Bell's theorem (15 subjects)
- Philosophical implications (10 subjects)

### Examples:

1. **The Quantum Superposition and the Problem of Measurement**
   - Schrödinger's cat
   - The superposition principle: |ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩
   - Collapse of the wave function
   - The measurement problem: why does measurement cause collapse?
   - Von Neumann's consciousness causes collapse theory

2. **Quantum Entanglement and the Non-Local Correlations in Reality**
   - Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox (1935)
   - "Spooky action at a distance"
   - Bell's theorem (1964)
   - Experimental violations of Bell inequalities (Aspect et al.)
   - No-signaling: cannot send information faster than light

3. **The Copenhagen Interpretation: Bohr and Heisenberg**
   - Complementarity principle
   - Wave-particle duality
   - The uncertainty principle: ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2
   - Positivist philosophy: only speak of observables
   - Critiques: Schrödinger, Einstein, Bell

4. **The Many-Worlds Interpretation (Everett)**
   - Hugh Everett III (1957)
   - No wave function collapse
   - The universe splits at each measurement
   - All outcomes occur in different branches
   - The preferred basis problem

5. **Bohmian Mechanics (Pilot-Wave Theory)**
   - David Bohm (1952)
   - Deterministic hidden variable theory
   - Particles have definite positions at all times
   - Guided by a pilot wave
   - Non-local but deterministic

6. **The Relational Interpretation (Rovelli)**
   - Carlo Rovelli
   - Quantum states are relative to observers
   - No absolute state of the system
   - Information-theoretic approach
   - Parallels with perspectivalism

7. **The Objective Collapse Theories (GRW)**
   - Ghirardi-Rimini-Weber model
   - Spontaneous collapse of wave function
   - Modification of Schrödinger equation
   - Collapse happens randomly
   - Avoids the measurement problem

8. **QBism (Quantum Bayesianism)**
   - Chris Fuchs, Rüdiger Schack
   - Quantum states represent subjective beliefs
   - Personalist Bayesian interpretation
   - Measurement updates beliefs
   - Participatory realism

9. **The Metaphysics of Quantum Properties**
   - Do particles have properties before measurement?
   - Realism vs. anti-realism about quantum states
   - Contextualism: properties depend on experimental context
   - The Kochen-Specker theorem
   - No non-contextual hidden variables

10. **Quantum Decoherence and the Classical Limit**
    - How does the classical world emerge?
    - Interaction with environment
    - Decoherence timescales
    - The preferred basis problem
    - Einselection (environment-induced superselection)
