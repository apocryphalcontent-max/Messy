### **I.A: Pure Actuality & First Principles** (90 subjects)

**Definition:** The investigation of *actus purus* as the absolute foundation of all reality - that which is utterly without potentiality, the Unmoved Mover, the uncaused cause of all contingent being.

**Philosophical Scope:** This is the centerpiece of Aristotelian-Thomistic metaphysics, addressing the necessity of a being whose essence is identical with its existence, containing no admixture of potentiality. It engages with the deepest questions of why there is something rather than nothing, and what must be true of the ultimate ground of being.

**Core Problems:**
- The convertibility of Pure Act with Being Itself (*Ipsum Esse Subsistens*)
- The relationship between Pure Actuality and Divine Simplicity
- The compatibility of Pure Act with divine freedom and personal attributes
- The metaphysical necessity of an Unmoved Mover in causal hierarchies

**Exemplary Entries:**

1. **"The Identity of Pure Actuality with the Unmoved Mover in Aristotelian-Thomistic Metaphysics"**
   - Analyzes Aristotle's Prime Mover (*Metaphysics* XII.7-9) and its development in Aquinas (*SCG* I.13, *ST* I, q.2, a.3)
   - Examines why the terminus of the causal chain must be actuality without potency
   - Addresses objections from Duns Scotus, Suárez, and modern process metaphysics

2. **"The Transcendental Convertibility of Being and Pure Act in Aquinas's *Actus Purus*"**
   - Explores the *De Ente et Essentia* distinction and its culmination in Pure Act
   - Analyzes how Pure Act grounds the analogy of being
   - Engages with *esse* as perfection rather than mere existence

3. **"The Problem of Divine Simplicity and the Multiplicity of Divine Attributes"**
   - Investigates the paradox: if God is absolutely simple, how can God possess multiple attributes?
   - Examines the Thomistic solution via identity of essence and attributes
   - Addresses Plantinga's, Wolterstorff's, and Bergmann's contemporary critiques

4. **"Duns Scotus's Critique of Pure Actuality and the Univocity of Being"**
   - Analyzes Scotus's rejection of the analogy of being in favor of univocity
   - Examines his alternative account of divine infinity as intensive magnitude
   - Assesses the metaphysical coherence of Scotus's system against Thomistic rebuttals

5. **"The Incompatibility of *Actus Purus* with Temporal Succession"**
   - Demonstrates why Pure Act entails eternity, not sempiternity
   - Engages with Augustine's *Confessions* XI on divine eternity
   - Addresses open theism and temporal process objections

6. **"Pure Actuality and the Grounding of Necessary Truths"**
   - Investigates whether mathematical and logical truths depend on God's intellect
   - Examines Aquinas, Descartes, and contemporary theistic conceptualism
   - Addresses the Euthyphro dilemma for necessary truths

7. **"The Divine Energies and the Palamite Critique of Pure Actuality"**
   - Examines Gregory Palamas's essence-energies distinction as alternative to *actus purus*
   - Analyzes the 14th-century Hesychast controversy
   - Evaluates compatibility between Eastern and Western metaphysics

8. **"Process Theology's Rejection of the Unmoved Mover"**
   - Analyzes Whitehead's dipolar God and Hartshorne's panentheism
   - Examines the critique that Pure Act is incompatible with divine love
   - Defends classical theism against process alternatives

9. **"The Principle of Proportionate Causality and Pure Actuality"**
   - Demonstrates why effects cannot exceed their total cause
   - Shows how Pure Act grounds all perfections found in creatures
   - Addresses the problem of imperfection arising from Perfect Being

10. **"Pure Actuality as the Foundation of Participated Esse"**
    - Analyzes how finite beings participate in *esse* without being *esse*
    - Examines Aquinas's metaphysics of participation
    - Engages with Neoplatonic influences and Christian transformations

### **I.B: The Ontological Difference & Ground** (75 subjects)

**Definition:** Heidegger's *Seinsfrage* - the distinction between Being itself (*Sein*) and particular beings (*Seiende*), and the investigation of what grounds this difference.

**Philosophical Scope:** This addresses the most fundamental question of metaphysics: why is there something rather than nothing? It examines the difference between the *that* of being and the *what* of beings, exploring the ground that makes possible the manifestation of any entity whatsoever.

**Core Problems:**
- The forgetfulness of Being (*Seinsvergessenheit*) in Western metaphysics
- The relationship between Being and time
- Whether Being has a ground, or whether the ground itself *is* Being
- The phenomenological disclosure of Being through *Dasein*

**Exemplary Entries:**

1. **"Heidegger's *Seinsfrage* and the Ontological Difference Between Being and Beings"**
   - Analyzes *Being and Time* §§1-4 on the question of the meaning of Being
   - Examines why Western metaphysics forgot Being by focusing only on beings
   - Addresses the circularity problem: must we already understand Being to ask about it?

2. **"The Ground of Grounding and the Metaphysical Foundation of All Foundation"**
   - Investigates Heidegger's *Der Satz vom Grund* (The Principle of Reason)
   - Examines whether the Principle of Sufficient Reason requires its own ground
   - Engages with Schelling's and Schopenhauer's accounts of groundless willing

3. **"The Abyss (*Abgrund*) and the Question of Whether Being Has a Why"**
   - Analyzes Meister Eckhart's "The rose is without why" in Heideggerian context
   - Examines *The Principle of Reason*: "Being: the abyss without ground"
   - Addresses mystical theology's convergence with phenomenological ontology

4. **"*Ereignis* and the Event of Appropriation in Late Heidegger"**
   - Investigates *Contributions to Philosophy* on the Event of Being
   - Examines the mutual appropriation of Being and *Dasein*
   - Analyzes the turn (*Kehre*) from Being and Time to later thought

5. **"The Fourfold (*das Geviert*) and the Gathering of Being"**
   - Examines "Building Dwelling Thinking" on earth, sky, divinities, mortals
   - Analyzes how the Fourfold allows things to "thing"
   - Addresses the quasi-theological implications of Heidegger's later thought

6. **"Aquinas's *Esse* and Heidegger's *Sein*: A Thomistic-Phenomenological Dialogue"**
   - Compares Aquinas's real distinction (essence/existence) with ontological difference
   - Examines whether Thomistic *esse* answers Heidegger's question
   - Addresses Gilson's and Fabro's Thomistic responses to Heidegger

7. **"The Nothing and the Problem of Negation in *What is Metaphysics?*"**
   - Analyzes Heidegger's claim that "the nothing nihilates" (*das Nichts nichtet*)
   - Examines Carnap's logical positivist critique
   - Defends the phenomenological intelligibility of the nothing

8. **"Being-toward-Death and the Ontological Structure of *Dasein*"**
   - Investigates *Being and Time* Division II on death as *Dasein's* ownmost possibility
   - Examines how anticipation of death discloses authentic Being
   - Addresses Levinas's ethical critique of Heideggerian mortality

9. **"The Ontotheological Constitution of Metaphysics"**
   - Analyzes "The Onto-theo-logical Constitution of Metaphysics" essay
   - Examines how Western metaphysics conflates Being with highest being (God)
   - Addresses implications for natural theology and philosophical theology

10. **"Aletheia (Unconcealment) and the Truth of Being"**
    - Investigates pre-Socratic *aletheia* vs. propositional *veritas*
    - Examines "On the Essence of Truth" on truth as disclosure
    - Addresses whether correspondence theory depends on unconcealment

### **I.C: Modalities of Existence** (75 subjects)

**Definition:** The metaphysical investigation of necessity, contingency, possibility, impossibility, and actuality - the ways in which beings can exist or fail to exist.

**Philosophical Scope:** Modal metaphysics addresses the structure of reality at its deepest level: what must be, what merely happens to be, what could be, and what cannot be. This engages with possible worlds semantics, essences, counterfactuals, and the very nature of modality itself.

**Core Problems:**
- Whether possible worlds exist and in what sense
- The relationship between necessity and essence
- The ontological status of merely possible objects
- De re vs. de dicto modality

**Exemplary Entries:**

1. **"The Modal Logic of Contingency and the Necessity of the Necessary Being"**
   - Analyzes S5 modal logic and the B (Brouwerian) axiom
   - Examines Hartshorne's and Malcolm's modal ontological arguments
   - Investigates whether "possibly necessary" entails "necessary"

2. **"Avicenna's Distinction Between Necessary and Contingent Existence"**
   - Examines *The Necessary Existent* (*wājib al-wujūd*) in Avicenna's metaphysics
   - Analyzes the *Metaphysics of the Healing* on essence-existence distinction
   - Assesses influence on Aquinas and Duns Scotus

3. **"David Lewis's Modal Realism and the Reality of Possible Worlds"**
   - Investigates *On the Plurality of Worlds* and concrete possible worlds
   - Examines the indexical theory of actuality
   - Addresses the incredulous stare objection and counterpart theory

4. **"Kripke's Possible Worlds Semantics and the Accessibility Relation"**
   - Analyzes *Naming and Necessity* on rigid designation
   - Examines frame semantics for modal logic systems
   - Investigates a posteriori necessities (water = H₂O)

5. **"Plantinga's Actualism and the Problem of Trans-World Identity"**
   - Examines "Actualism and Possible Worlds" on maximal states of affairs
   - Analyzes the critique of Lewis's modal realism
   - Investigates individual essences as world-indexed properties

6. **"Aristotelian Potentiality and the Metaphysics of Real Possibility"**
   - Examines *Metaphysics* Θ (Theta) on *dynamis* and *energeia*

7. **"Absolute vs. Relative Necessity in Divine and Created Being"**
   - Investigates Aquinas's *necessitas absoluta* vs. *necessitas ex suppositione*
   - Examines whether created beings have any intrinsic necessity
   - Addresses the problem of natural laws as necessary or contingent

8. **"The Principle of Plenitude and Leibniz's Best of All Possible Worlds"**
   - Analyzes *Theodicy* and the optimality thesis
   - Examines whether all genuine possibilities must be actualized
   - Addresses Voltaire's critique in *Candide*

9. **"Logical Possibility vs. Metaphysical Possibility"**
   - Investigates the distinction between conceivability and possibility
   - Examines Chalmers's two-dimensional semantics

### **I.D: Negative Theology & Apophatic Being** (80 subjects)

**Definition:** The theological and philosophical tradition asserting that God, or ultimate reality, transcends all positive predication - knowable only through negation of creaturely attributes.

**Philosophical Scope:** Apophatic theology addresses the fundamental limits of human language and conceptuality when confronting absolute transcendence. It investigates whether affirmative statements about God are ever literally true, or whether all theological language requires via negativa, via eminentiae, or analogical qualification.

**Core Problems:**
- Whether apophaticism leads to agnosticism or mystical knowledge
- The relationship between negative theology and Neoplatonic henology
- The compatibility of apophaticism with Biblical revelation
- Whether affirmative theology is ever possible

**Exemplary Entries:**

1. **"Pseudo-Dionysius's *Via Negativa* and the Divine Darkness Beyond Being"**

   - Examines the "dazzling darkness" and "brilliant obscurity"

   - Addresses the 1329 papal condemnation and Eckhart's orthodoxy

3. **"Gregory of Nyssa's Infinite Progress (*Epektasis*) in the Divine Darkness"**
   - Investigates *The Life of Moses* on perpetual ascent
   - Examines how infinity of God means inexhaustibility, not lack
   - Analyzes the "luminous darkness" of Sinai

4. **"Maimonides's Negative Attributes and the *Via Negativa* in Jewish Philosophy"**

   - Investigates Aquinas's response to Maimonidean negative theology

5. **"The Cloud of Unknowing and Apophatic Contemplation in Christian Mysticism"**
   - Investigates 14th-century English mysticism's naked intent unto God

   - Analyzes the influence of Pseudo-Dionysius on the work

6. **"Maximus the Confessor's Synthesis of Kataphatic and Apophatic Theology"**
   - Examines *Ambigua* on the complementarity of affirmation and negation

7. **"Sankara's *Neti Neti* (Not This, Not This) and Advaita Vedanta Apophaticism"**
   - Examines the method of progressive negation in *Brahmasutra Bhasya*
   - Analyzes *nirguna Brahman* (Brahman without attributes)
   - Compares with Christian apophaticism (convergences and divergences)

8. **"The One Beyond Being in Plotinus's *Enneads* V.1-V.5"**

### **I.E: The Primordial Unity & First Distinction** (80 subjects)

- Whether the One is beyond Being or identical with it
- The mechanism of the first procession (emanation, creation, logical necessity)

- The relationship between the One and the Divine Persons (Christian theology)

### **I.F: The Transcendental Convertibility** (75 subjects)

**Definition:** The scholastic doctrine that Being, Truth, Goodness, Beauty, and Unity are convertible (*convertuntur*) - that to be is to be true, good, beautiful, and one.

**Philosophical Scope:** This addresses the fundamental structure of reality: are the transcendentals distinct properties or different aspects of the same reality? This engages with Platonic participation, Aristotelian categories, and Thomistic metaphysics.

**Core Problems:**
- Whether the transcendentals are really distinct or only conceptually distinct
- The relationship between the transcendentals and Being itself
- Whether beauty is a transcendental or only a property of some beings
- The ontological ground of truth and goodness

**Exemplary Entries:**

1. **"The Identity of Being, Truth, and Goodness in the Transcendental Order"**
   - Analyzes Aquinas *De Veritate* q.1, a.1 on *ens et verum convertuntur*
   - Examines the ontological foundation of truth in being
   - Investigates whether goodness is coextensive with being

2. **"Beauty as the Radiant Transcendental Attracting Intellect and Will"**
   - Examines Pseudo-Dionysius's *Divine Names* on beauty as splendor of form
   - Analyzes Aquinas *ST* I, q.5, a.4 ad 1: beauty as *claritas*, *integritas*, *proportio*
   - Investigates Von Balthasar's *The Glory of the Lord* on theological aesthetics

3. **"The Transcendental Properties of Being in Aquinas's *De Veritate*"**
   - Examines q.1 (truth), q.21 (goodness), q.22 (unity)
   - Analyzes why the transcendentals express being's intelligibility
   - Investigates how they differ from categorical accidents

4. **"The Problem of the 'Missing Transcendental': Why Is Being Not Listed?"**
   - Examines scholastic lists: *unum*, *verum*, *bonum* (sometimes *pulchrum*)
   - Analyzes why *ens* (being) is presupposed rather than listed

5. **"Duns Scotus's *Haecceity* and the Transcendental of 'Thisness'"**
   - Examines whether *haecceitas* (individual essence) is a transcendental
   - Analyzes the debate over individuation principles
   - Investigates the metaphysics of individuation in Scotus

6. **"The Platonic Triad: Being, Life, Intelligence and the Transcendentals"**
   - Examines Neoplatonic triad (*on*, *zoe*, *nous*)
   - Analyzes Plotinus *Enneads* VI.7-VI.9 on the transcendentals of the *nous*
   - Investigates influence on Christian metaphysics

7. **"Al-Farabi and Avicenna on the Transcendental Properties in Islamic Philosophy"**
   - Examines *al-Sifāʾ* (The Healing) on being, unity, truth
   - Analyzes the relationship between *wujud* (existence) and *mahiyyah* (quiddity)
   - Investigates influence on Latin scholasticism

8. **"The Convertibility of *Ens* and *Unum*: Unity as Undividedness"**
   - Analyzes Aquinas *ST* I, q.11, a.1: "One and Being are convertible"

10. **"Von Balthasar's Retrieval of Beauty as a Central Transcendental"**

    - Analyzes the relationship between beauty and divine revelation
    - Investigates the transcendental aesthetics of the Christ form
