#### **1. "Cantor's Transfinite Cardinals and the Hierarchy of Infinities"**

**Abstract:** This entry examines Georg Cantor's revolutionary discovery that infinity admits of degrees - that there are infinitely many distinct sizes of infinity, organized in a well-ordered hierarchy. We analyze Cantor's diagonal argument proving *℘(ℵ₀) > ℵ₀*, the continuum hypothesis, and the philosophical implications for Platonism, theological conceptions of the infinite, and the nature of mathematical reality.

**I. Historical Context: The Crisis of the Infinite**

Prior to Cantor, mainstream mathematics treated infinity with suspicion. Aristotle's distinction between *potential* infinity (endless process) and *actual* infinity (completed totality) dominated for two millennia. Gauss declared: "I protest against the use of infinite magnitude as something completed, which is never permissible in mathematics." Cantor's genius was to make actual infinity mathematically tractable.

**II. The Diagonal Argument and Non-Denumerability**

Cantor's 1891 diagonal proof demonstrates that the real numbers ℝ cannot be put in one-to-one correspondence with the natural numbers ℕ. The proof structure:

1. Assume, for contradiction, that ℝ is countable
2. List all reals in [0,1]: r₁, r₂, r₃, ...
3. Construct a new real r* differing from rₙ at the nth decimal place
4. r* is real but not in the list - contradiction

**Philosophical Implications:**
- *Ontological*: If sets are discovered, not invented, Cantor revealed objective structure to infinity
- *Epistemological*: We can have *a priori* knowledge of infinite totalities via proof
- *Theological*: Does this vindicate Gregory of Nyssa's *epektasis* - eternal progress in the infinite God?

**III. The Continuum Hypothesis and Mathematical Incompleteness**

Cantor conjectured: There is no cardinal strictly between *ℵ₀* (countable) and *2^ℵ₀* (cardinality of continuum). Hilbert listed this as Problem #1 (1900). Gödel (1938) proved CH is consistent with ZFC. Cohen (1963) proved ¬CH is also consistent. **Result:** CH is *independent* of ZFC axioms.

**Implications for Mathematical Realism:**
- Platonist view (Gödel): CH has a determinate truth value; we simply haven't discovered it yet
- Formalist view: CH is meaningless outside an axiom system
- Multiverse view (Hamkins): CH is true in some set-theoretic universes, false in others

**IV. The Absolute Infinite and Theological Dimensions**

Cantor distinguished:
- *Transfinitum* (transfinite numbers: ℵ₀, ℵ₁, ...)
- *Absolutum* (Absolute Infinite, which cannot be increased)

In correspondence with Catholic theologians, Cantor identified the Absolute Infinite with God. He wrote: "The transfinite with its abundance of formations and forms, points to an Absolute, to the 'true Infinite,' whose magnitude is not capable of either increase or decrease, and is therefore to be regarded quantitatively as an absolute maximum."

**Thomistic Analysis:** Does this align with divine infinity? Aquinas argues God's infinity is *intensive* (perfection without limit), not *extensive* (quantity without end). Cantor's Absolute seems quantitative, yet beyond all quantity.

**V. Critiques and Paradoxes**

**Burali-Forti Paradox (1897):** Consider the set of all ordinal numbers. This set has an ordinal number, which must be the largest ordinal. But the successor of any ordinal is also an ordinal - contradiction.

**Cantor's Paradox (1899):** Consider the set of all sets *V*. By Cantor's theorem, *℘(V) > |V|*. But *℘(V) ⊆ V* (powerset is a set of sets). Contradiction.

**Resolution:** Von Neumann-Bernays-Gödel set theory distinguishes *sets* from *proper classes*. The ordinals and *V* are proper classes, not sets.

**VI. Contemporary Developments**

- **Large Cardinals:** Inaccessible, measurable, Woodin cardinals push beyond ZFC
- **Hyperintensional Contexts:** How do modal operators interact with infinity?
- **Physical Infinity:** Does spacetime have infinite points, or is it discrete (loop quantum gravity)?

**VII. Synthesis: The Perennial Philosophy of Infinity**

The mathematical infinite illuminates the theological infinite:
- *Apophatic Theology*: Just as Absolute Infinite transcends all transfinite numbers, God transcends all creatures
- *Participatory Metaphysics*: Finite beings participate in infinite Being, as finite numbers participate in infinite sets
- *Mystical Union*: Gregory of Nyssa's eternal ascent mirrors the endless hierarchy ℵ₀, ℵ₁, ℵ₂, ...

#### **2. "Zeno's Paradoxes and the Problem of the Continuum"**

**Abstract:** Zeno of Elea's paradoxes (5th c. BCE) challenge the intelligibility of motion, plurality, and the infinite divisibility of space and time. We examine the classical paradoxes (Achilles, Dichotomy, Arrow, Stadium), their relationship to Parmenides's monism, and contemporary resolutions via calculus, measure theory, and quantum mechanics. The entry demonstrates how ancient puzzles remain live issues in metaphysics and physics.

**I. Historical Context: Parmenides and the Denial of Multiplicity**

Zeno was a disciple of Parmenides, who argued:
- Being is one, continuous, indivisible, unchanging
- Motion and multiplicity are illusions
- Sense experience is deceptive; only *logos* (reason) grasps truth

Zeno's paradoxes were designed as *reductio ad absurdum* arguments: if you accept motion/multiplicity, contradictions follow; therefore, reject motion/multiplicity.

**II. The Four Classical Paradoxes**

### **A. The Dichotomy (Division)**

*To reach a destination, you must first traverse half the distance. But before that, you must traverse half of that half. And so on, ad infinitum. Thus, motion cannot even begin.*

**Formal Structure:**
- Distance D requires passing D/2, D/4, D/8, ..., D/2ⁿ, ...
- This is an infinite sequence of tasks
- One cannot complete infinitely many tasks in finite time
- ∴ Motion is impossible

**Contemporary Analysis:**
1. **Calculus Resolution:** Infinite sums can converge. ∑(1/2ⁿ) = 1. So infinite tasks can take finite time.
2. **Objection (Thomson's Lamp):** But how does the *last* step occur? There is no last term in ∑(1/2ⁿ).
3. **Response:** Motion is continuous, not discrete. There are no discrete "steps" - this is a category error.

### **B. Achilles and the Tortoise**

*Achilles races a tortoise with a head start. When Achilles reaches where the tortoise was, the tortoise has advanced. When Achilles reaches that new position, the tortoise has advanced again. Ad infinitum. Achilles never overtakes.*

**Formal Structure:**
- Time tₙ: Achilles reaches tortoise's position at tₙ₋₁
- But tortoise is now at position pₙ (ahead of Achilles)
- ∴ Achilles is always behind

**Analysis:**
- **Mathematical Error:** Zeno conflates *infinite divisibility* with *infinite duration*
- **Resolution:** Achilles overtakes at time t* = limit of the sequence {tₙ}
- **Deeper Issue:** Does time consist of *instants* (Dedekind cuts) or *intervals* (extended presents)?

### **C. The Arrow**

*At any instant, an arrow in flight occupies a space equal to itself. If it occupies a space equal to itself, it is at rest. Therefore, at every instant, the arrow is at rest. But if it is at rest at every instant, it is always at rest. ∴ Motion is impossible.*

**Formal Structure:**
- At instant t: arrow is at position p(t)
- To be "moving" at t requires being at multiple positions at t
- But that is contradictory
- ∴ Arrow is at rest at t

**Contemporary Analysis:**
1. **At-At Theory of Motion:** Motion is not a property *at* an instant, but a property *across* instants. An object moves iff it is at different positions at different times. (Russell, Carnap)
2. **Objection:** This dissolves rather than solves the problem. It stipulates motion rather than explaining it.
3. **Aristotelian Response:** Motion is *actuality of potentiality qua potentiality*. At instant t, the arrow is *actually* at p(t) and *potentially* at p(t+dt). Motion is the reality of potency.

### **D. The Stadium**

*Three rows of objects: A (stationary), B (moving right), C (moving left). From A's perspective, B passes one object per unit time. But from B's perspective, C passes two objects per unit time. This implies time is both discrete and continuous - contradiction.*

**Modern Irrelevance:** This paradox exploits a confusion about reference frames, now clarified by Galilean relativity.

**III. The Measure-Theoretic Perspective**

Zeno's paradoxes assume:
1. Space/time are infinitely divisible
2. Points have zero extension
3. Summing zero-extension points should yield zero

**Measure Theory Response:** The Lebesgue measure of an interval [0,1] is 1, even though it contains uncountably many points of measure 0. *Continuous magnitudes are not composed of points* but are irreducibly continuous.

**Whitehead's Alternative:** In *Process and Reality*, Whitehead proposes an *extensive continuum* where regions are primary, and points are derivative abstractions. There is no "composition" of points into intervals.

**IV. Quantum Mechanics and Discrete Space**

**Loop Quantum Gravity:** Space is quantized. Minimum length: Planck length (10⁻³⁵ m). Space is a spin network, not a continuum.

**Implications for Zeno:**
- If space is discrete, Dichotomy fails: there is a finite number of steps
- But: Quantum mechanics preserves wave-function continuity

**Debate:** Is spacetime fundamentally discrete or continuous? (String theory: continuous; LQG: discrete)

**V. The Persistence Problem**

**Endurantism vs. Perdurantism:**
- *Endurantism*: Objects are wholly present at each time
- *Perdurantism*: Objects are four-dimensional, with temporal parts

**Zeno's Arrow and Persistence:**
- If endurantism is true, the Arrow paradox is vindicated: nothing is "happening" at an instant
- If perdurantism is true, motion is the sequence of temporal parts at different locations

**VI. Contemporary Variants**

**Grünbaum's Infinity Machine:** Imagine a lamp toggled on/off infinitely many times in finite time. Is it on or off at the end? (Answer: undefined - but this doesn't refute the possibility of completing infinitely many tasks; it refutes the task *description*.)

**Black's Marbles:** Infinitely many marbles transferred between urns. Where are they at t = 1? (Various responses: mass-energy conservation violations, stipulation absurdity)

**VII. Thomistic-Aristotelian Synthesis**

**Aquinas on the Continuum (*In Phys.* VI):**
- A continuum is not composed of indivisibles (points/instants)
- Motion is not a series of discrete states but a unified act
- *Actus imperfecti* - the actuality of the potential insofar as it is potential

**Resolution of Zeno:**
- Motion is not reducible to positions-at-times
- Motion is ontologically primitive
- The infinite divisibility is *potential*, not *actual*

**VIII. Conclusion: The Enduring Mystery**

2,500 years later, Zeno's paradoxes remain philosophically live:
- Is the continuum composed of points?
- Is time composed of instants?
- What is the ontological status of motion?

**Mathematical resolution** (calculus) does not equal **metaphysical resolution** (nature of change).

#### **3. "Aristotle's Potential vs. Actual Infinity and the Limit Concept"**

**Abstract:** Aristotle's *Physics* III.4-8 establishes the distinction between potential infinity (endless process, never completed) and actual infinity (completed totality). This distinction dominated mathematics until Cantor. We examine Aristotle's arguments, his motivations (avoiding paradox), and whether modern mathematics has refuted or vindicated his position.

**I. Aristotle's Definition of Infinity**

**Physics 206b:** "The infinite is that of which, as it is counted or measured, there is always something outside."

- *Key Insight*: Infinity is not a maximal quantity but an inexhaustibility
- Infinity is always *in process*, never *complete*

**II. The Rejection of Actual Infinity**

**Argument 1: No Infinite Body**
- If an infinite body existed, it would either be:
  - Homogeneous: then it would displace all finite bodies (absurd)
  - Heterogeneous: then it would have parts of different natures (incoherent)
- ∴ No infinite body exists

**Argument 2: No Infinite Number**
- Every number is numerable (countable in principle)
- An infinite number cannot be numbered
- ∴ No infinite number

**Argument 3: No Infinite Past**
- If the past were infinite, we could not have arrived at the present (traversal problem)
- ∴ The universe has a finite age

**III. Potential Infinity: The Intelligible Infinite**

**Examples:**
1. **Division of a magnitude:** You can always divide further - but the process never terminates in actual points
2. **Addition of numbers:** You can always add 1 - but you never reach "infinity"
3. **Time:** The future is potentially infinite (endless) but never actually infinite (never a final moment)

**Metaphysical Ground:** Potential infinity is grounded in *matter* (that which can always be further determined).

**IV. Modern Mathematics and the Rehabilitation of Actual Infinity**

**Dedekind (1888):** Defined infinite set: A set S is infinite iff S is equinumerous with a proper subset of itself.

**Cantor's Actualism:** Transfinite numbers *are* completed totalities. ℵ₀ is an actual number.

**Hilbert's Defense:** "No one shall expel us from the Paradise that Cantor has created."

**Question:** Has mathematics *refuted* Aristotle, or merely *changed the subject*?

**V. The Concept of Limit: Vindicating Aristotle?**

**Limit of a sequence:** lim (aₙ) = L means:
- For every ε > 0, there exists N such that |aₙ - L| < ε for all n > N

**Key:** The limit is *not* a term in the sequence. It is the value *approached* but *never reached* by the sequence.

**This is potential infinity:** The sequence approaches arbitrarily close but never "completes" at infinity.

**Gauge Theory (Cauchy):** Similarly, we never "reach" the infinitesimal dx - it is always potential, never actual.

**VI. Contemporary Debate: Actual Infinity in Physics**

**Cosmology:**
- Is the universe spatially infinite? (Evidence: flat geometry suggests yes)
- Is the past eternal? (Big Bang suggests finite past; cyclic models suggest infinite)

**Black Holes:**
- Do singularities involve actual infinities (infinite density)?
- Are infinities a sign that GR breaks down (replaced by quantum gravity)?

**Infinity as Physical vs. Mathematical:**
- Penrose: Physical infinities are probably unreal
- Mathematical infinities are idealized tools

**VII. Thomistic Integration**

**Aquinas (*ST* I, q.7, a.4):**
- God's infinity is *intensive* (perfection), not *extensive* (quantity)
- Actual quantitative infinity is impossible in created being
- Potential infinity is real (endless divisibility, endless duration)

**Synthesis:** Aristotle is correct about *physical* infinity; Cantor is correct about *mathematical* infinity. These are distinct domains.

#### **1. "The Liar Paradox and the Limits of Truth-Functional Semantics"**

**Abstract:** "This sentence is false." If true, it's false. If false, it's true. The Liar Paradox has plagued logic since antiquity, challenging the coherence of the concept of truth. We examine classical solutions (Tarski's hierarchy, Kripke's fixed points, paraconsistent acceptance), the Strengthened Liar, Revenge problems, and implications for deflationism, correspondence theory, and the very project of formal semantics.

**I. Ancient Formulations**

**Epimenides the Cretan (6th c. BCE):** "All Cretans are liars."
- If true, Epimenides is a liar, so it's false
- If false, not all Cretans are liars, so it could be true
- (Weakness: This is not a pure Liar - Epimenides could be the exception)

**Eubulides of Miletus (4th c. BCE):** "I am lying." (Pure form)

**St. Paul (Titus 1:12):** "One of Crete's own prophets has said it: 'Cretans are always liars...'"
- Medieval scholastics debated: Is Paul endorsing a paradox?

**II. The Classical Liar: Semantic Structure**

**Formalization:**
Let L = "L is false"

1. Assume L is true
2. Then what L says is the case
3. L says L is false
4. So L is false (contradiction with 1)
5. Assume L is false
6. Then what L says is not the case
7. L says L is false
8. So L is not false, i.e., true (contradiction with 5)
9. ∴ L is neither true nor false (?) or both (?)

**III. Tarski's Hierarchy of Languages**

**Tarski's Undefinability Theorem (1933):** Truth for a language L cannot be defined within L itself.

**Solution:** Metalanguages.
- L₀: Object language (no truth predicate)
- L₁: Metalanguage (contains truth predicate for L₀)
- L₂: Meta-metalanguage (contains truth predicate for L₁)
- ...

**The Liar sentence:** "This sentence is false" is *meaningless* because "false" must be indexed to a level.

**Objection - The Strengthened Liar:** "This sentence is not true in any metalanguage."
- Tarski's hierarchy is *indexed*, but the Strengthened Liar is *absolutized*

**Revenge:** Any solution that makes the Liar meaningless faces: "This sentence is meaningless" - which generates the same paradox.

**IV. Kripke's Fixed-Point Semantics (1975)**

**Three-Valued Logic:** T (true), F (false), U (undefined)

**Key Idea:** Build truth "from below" via fixed-point iteration.

1. Start with classical valuations (ground sentences: T or F)
2. Extend to sentences involving truth predicate
3. Iterate until fixed point (no new valuations)
4. The Liar receives value U (undefined)

**Advantages:**
- Natural handling of grounded vs. ungrounded sentences
- Allows sentences about truth without metalanguages

**Disadvantages:**
- The Liar is *undefined*, but "The Liar is undefined" generates a new paradox
- Requires rejection of bivalence (Law of Excluded Middle)

**V. Paraconsistent Approach: Dialetheism (Priest)**

**Radical Solution:** Accept the contradiction. The Liar is *both true and false*.

**Dialetheism:** Some contradictions are true. There are *true contradictions* (dialetheia).

**Formal Logic:** LP (Logic of Paradox) - a paraconsistent logic where:
- Contradictions can be true
- But explosion (⊤ → ⊥) does not follow

**Advantages:**
- No need for truth-value gaps
- No need for metalanguage hierarchy
- The Liar is simply a true contradiction

**Disadvantages:**
- Violates Law of Non-Contradiction (LNC)
- Threatens rationality itself (if LNC is negotiable, what isn't?)
- "Revenge" liars still arise: "This sentence is *only* false"

**VI. Contextualist Solutions (Barwise & Etchemendy)**

**Situation Semantics:** Truth is relative to *situations*.

**The Liar:** "This sentence is false" involves a self-referential situation that contains no complete valuation.

**Parallel:** Russell's paradox resolved via ZF's Foundation axiom (no set contains itself). The Liar involves a "situation" that contains itself illicitly.

**VII. Deflationary vs. Robust Truth**

**Deflationary Theory:** Truth is merely disquotation. "Snow is white" is true iff snow is white.

**Problem:** The Liar threatens deflationism. If "L is true" ≡ L, then:
- "L is true" ≡ "L is false"
- Contradiction

**Field's Response:** The disquotation schema must be *restricted* to exclude pathological cases.

**Objection:** This is *ad hoc*. We've lost the simplicity of deflationism.

**VIII. Philosophical Implications**

**For Correspondence Theory:**
- If truth is correspondence to reality, what does the Liar correspond to?
- The Liar seems *semantic* (about language), not *metaphysical* (about world)

**For Coherence Theory:**
- The Liar is maximally *incoherent*
- Does this mean it's false? But saying it's false regenerates the paradox.

**For Pragmatic Theory:**
- The Liar has no practical use
- But does uselessness equal falsity?

**IX. Theological Dimensions: Omniscience and the Liar**

**Problem:** Can God know the truth value of "This sentence is false"?

**Options:**
1. God knows it's true (but then it's false)
2. God knows it's false (but then it's true)
3. God knows it's neither (but then truth is incomplete)
4. God knows it's both (but then LNC fails even for God)
5. God knows the Liar is *ill-formed* (but can God fail to know something well-formed?)

**Thomistic Response:** God knows all *intelligible* truths. The Liar is not a genuine proposition (it's pseudo-meaningful).

**Objection:** This seems to limit omniscience.

**X. Conclusion: The Liar as Perennial Problem**

No consensus solution exists after 2,500 years. This suggests:
1. The problem is *deep* (not merely technical)
2. It may require *metaphysical* (not just logical) resolution
3. It challenges our most basic intuitions about truth, meaning, and rationality

#### **2. "Russell's Paradox and the Foundations of Set Theory"**

**Abstract:** Russell's Paradox (1901) devastated Frege's logicist program by showing that naive set theory is contradictory. The paradox asks: Does the set of all sets that are not members of themselves, contain itself? We examine the paradox, its impact on Frege, Zermelo-Fraenkel's axiomatic response, type theory, and implications for Platonism in mathematics.

**I. The Genesis of the Paradox**

**Naive Set Comprehension:** For any property P, there exists a set {x : P(x)}.

**Russell's Discovery:** Let R = {x : x ∉ x} (the set of all sets that are not members of themselves).

**Question:** Is R ∈ R?

1. Assume R ∈ R
2. Then R satisfies the defining property: R ∉ R
3. Contradiction
4. Assume R ∉ R
5. Then R satisfies the defining property: R ∈ R
6. Contradiction
7. ∴ Naive set theory is inconsistent

**II. The Destruction of Frege's Logicism**

**Frege's *Grundgesetze der Arithmetik* (1893, 1903):** Aimed to reduce arithmetic to logic via Basic Law V:
- The extension of concept F = the extension of concept G iff ∀x(Fx ↔ Gx)

**Russell's Letter (1902):** Informed Frege of the paradox.

**Frege's Response:** "A scientist can hardly encounter anything more undesirable than to have the foundation collapse just as the work is finished. I was put in this position by a letter from Mr. Bertrand Russell..."

**Frege's Attempted Fix:** Restrict Basic Law V. (Failed - led to new inconsistencies)

**III. Zermelo-Fraenkel Set Theory (ZFC): The Axiomatic Solution**

**Key Innovations:**
1. **Axiom of Separation (Subset Axiom):** You cannot form arbitrary sets, only subsets of existing sets
   - {x ∈ A : P(x)} is a set (for any set A and property P)
   - Russell's R cannot be formed: R = {x ∈ ??? : x ∉ x} - there's no "universe set" to draw from

2. **No Universal Set:** There is no set of all sets

3. **Axiom of Foundation (Regularity):** No set is a member of itself, even indirectly
   - Prevents infinite descending chains: ... ∈ xₙ ∈ ... ∈ x₂ ∈ x₁ ∈ x₀

**Result:** ZFC is consistent (relative to arithmetic) and avoids Russell's Paradox.

**IV. Type Theory: Russell's Own Solution**

**Ramified Type Theory (Principia Mathematica, 1910-1913):**

**Hierarchy:**
- Individuals: Type 0
- Sets of individuals: Type 1
- Sets of sets of individuals: Type 2
- ...

**Rule:** x ∈ y only if type(x) < type(y)

**Russell's R:** R = {x : x ∉ x} is *meaningless* because "x ∈ x" violates type restrictions.

**Advantage:** Paradox-free.

**Disadvantage:**
- Mathematically cumbersome
- Requires Axiom of Reducibility (eliminates the entire point of ramification!)

**V. Alternative Set Theories**

**Quine's New Foundations (NF, 1937):**
- Allows {x : x ∉ x}, but stratification prevents contradiction
- Highly unintuitive (|℘(X)| = |X| for infinite X)

**Von Neumann-Bernays-Gödel (NBG):**
- Distinguishes *sets* from *proper classes*
- Russell's R is a proper class, not a set
- No contradiction

**Paraconsistent Set Theory:**
- Accept the contradiction (Priest's *Inconsistent Mathematics*)
- Use paraconsistent logic to avoid explosion

**VI. Philosophical Implications**

**For Mathematical Platonism:**
- If sets are discovered, not invented, why did our initial "discovery" lead to contradiction?
- Does this suggest sets are *constructed* (conventionalism)?

**For Logicism:**
- Russell's Paradox killed Frege's logicism
- Is mathematics reducible to logic? (Consensus: No.)

**For Formalism:**
- Hilbert: Mathematics is a formal game of symbol manipulation
- Russell's Paradox shows we need metamathematical consistency proofs
- Gödel (1931): No finitary proof of consistency is possible

**VII. Cantor's Paradox and the Absolute Infinite**

**Cantor's Theorem:** For any set S, |℘(S)| > |S|.

**Application to V (universe of all sets):** |℘(V)| > |V|. But ℘(V) ⊆ V. Contradiction.

**Cantor's Solution:** V is not a set; it's the *Absolute Infinite*, which transcends set theory.

**Parallel:** God transcends creation. You cannot form "the set of all created beings" because that would make creation itself a member of creation.

**VIII. The Barber Paradox (Russell's Popularization)**

*In a village, the barber shaves all and only those who do not shave themselves. Does the barber shave himself?*

**Analysis:**
- If yes, then he's one who shaves himself, so he doesn't shave himself (contradiction)
- If no, then he's one who doesn't shave himself, so he does shave himself (contradiction)

**Solution:** The barber does not exist. This is a proof by contradiction that the village is impossible.

**Lesson:** Self-reference creates paradox. Restrict it via types or axioms.

**IX. Curry's Paradox and the Threat to Relevance Logic**

**Curry's Paradox:** Let C = "If C is true, then the moon is made of cheese."

1. Assume C is true
2. Then "If C is true, then the moon is made of cheese" is true
3. By modus ponens: the moon is made of cheese
4. ∴ C → (moon is cheese)
5. ∴ C is true (by conditional proof)
6. ∴ The moon is made of cheese

**Moral:** Even relevance logic (which restricts implication to relevant premises) struggles with Curry.

**X. Conclusion: Russell's Paradox as Watershed**

Russell's Paradox forced:
1. Axiomatization of set theory (ZFC)
2. Recognition that not every property determines a set
3. Abandonment of unrestricted comprehension
4. Shift from naive to critical foundations

**It remains the paradigm case of why *foundational rigor matters*.**
