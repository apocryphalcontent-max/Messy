## CLXXI: THE ORTHODOX BIBLICAL EXEGESIS (3,000 subjects)

### **METHODOLOGICAL PROLEGOMENA**

**The Orthodox Hermeneutical Framework:**

1. **The Fourfold Sense of Scripture (John Cassian, *Conferences* 14.8):**
   - *Literal/Historical* (*historia*): The plain meaning of the text
   - *Allegorical* (*allegoria*): Typological meaning (OT → NT, earthly → heavenly)
   - *Tropological/Moral* (*tropologia*): Application to the soul's moral life
   - *Anagogical* (*anagoge*): Eschatological/mystical meaning

2. **The Primacy of Tradition (St. Irenaeus, *Against Heresies* 3.2.2):**
   - Scripture cannot be interpreted apart from the Church's living Tradition
   - The Rule of Faith (*regula fidei*) provides the hermeneutical key
   - Private interpretation is rejected (cf. 2 Peter 1:20)

3. **Christocentric Reading:**
   - All Scripture finds its fulfillment in Christ (Luke 24:27, 44-47)
   - The Old Testament is a shadow; Christ is the reality (Col. 2:17)
   - Typology: Adam → Christ, Eve → Mary, Israel → Church, Passover → Eucharist

4. **The Inspiration and Authority of the LXX:**
   - The Septuagint (LXX) is authoritative, not merely a translation
   - The Apostles quote from the LXX (not Masoretic Text)
   - Differences from Hebrew MT are theologically significant

#### **1. "Genesis 1:1-3 - *Bereshit Bara Elohim*: The Trinity in Creation and the Logos as Arche"**

**ABSTRACT:** The opening verses of Genesis contain the foundational revelation of *creatio ex nihilo*, the Trinitarian structure of God's creative act, and the proto-incarnational presence of the divine Logos. Orthodox exegesis, following Basil the Great's *Hexaemeron*, Athanasius's *Contra Gentes*, and John Chrysostom's homilies, identifies the plural "us" in v.26, the Spirit hovering in v.2, and the Word speaking in v.3 as Trinitarian disclosure. This entry examines the Hebrew/Greek linguistic issues, Patristic consensus, Jewish interpretations (Rashi, Maimonides, Philo), and contemporary theological implications for cosmology, ecology, and the Incarnation.

---

**SECTION I: THE TEXT - SEPTUAGINT vs. MASORETIC**

**Hebrew (Masoretic Text):**
בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים אֵת הַשָּׁמַיִם וְאֵת הָאָרֶץ
*Bereshit bara Elohim et hashamayim ve'et ha'aretz*

**Greek (Septuagint):**
Ἐν ἀρχῇ ἐποίησεν ὁ θεὸς τὸν οὐρανὸν καὶ τὴν γῆν
*En archē epoiēsen ho theos ton ouranon kai tēn gēn*

**Critical Differences:**
1. **בְּרֵאשִׁית (*bereshit*) vs. Ἐν ἀρχῇ (*en archē*):**
   - Hebrew: "In beginning" (construct state, no definite article)
   - Greek: "In *the* beginning" (definite article present)
   - **Patristic Significance:** *Archē* (ἀρχή) connects to John 1:1 (*En archē ēn ho Logos*), identifying Christ as the *Archē* (Beginning/Principle) through whom creation occurs

2. **בָּרָא (*bara*) vs. ἐποίησεν (*epoiēsen*):**
   - Hebrew: *bara* (used only of divine creation, never with preexisting material)
   - Greek: *epoiēsen* (made/did, from *poieō*)
   - Both indicate *creation*, not *formation* from preexisting matter

3. **אֱלֹהִים (*Elohim*) - Plural Form:**
   - Grammatically plural, but takes singular verb (*bara*, not *bar'u*)
   - Jewish interpretation: Pluralis maiestatis (plural of majesty)
   - Christian interpretation: Proto-Trinitarian (plurality in unity)

**SECTION II: PATRISTIC EXEGESIS - THE FATHERS ON GENESIS 1:1**

### **A. St. Basil the Great - *Hexaemeron* (Nine Homilies on Creation, c. 370 AD)**

**Homily I.1-2: "In the Beginning"**

**Basil's Key Arguments:**

1. **Temporal Beginning vs. Logical Principle:**
   - "Some understand 'in the beginning' as a temporal beginning... others as the principle (*archē*) which is the Wisdom of God, through whom all things were made."
   - Basil synthesizes both: Creation had a temporal beginning AND occurred through the Logos-Wisdom

2. **Against Eternal Matter (Greek Cosmogony):**
   - Refutes Platonic *Timaeus*: The Demiurge did not shape preexisting matter
   - *Bara* (create) implies bringing into existence from non-existence
   - "What was there before the creation? ... Nothing. For if there had been anything, it would not be a beginning."

3. **The Simplicity of Moses's Account:**
   - Moses's account is pedagogically simple yet metaphysically profound
   - Avoids Greek philosophical jargon to communicate to all humanity
   - The depth is discovered through spiritual interpretation, not rejected by literalism

**Homily I.5: The Work of the First Day**

- The "light" of Day 1 (v.3) is *not* the sun (created Day 4)
- Patristic consensus: This is *uncreated light*, the divine energies, the glory of God
- Parallel: The Tabor Light in the Transfiguration (Matt 17:2)

**Theological Synthesis:**
- Creation is an act of divine *will* and *wisdom*
- The cosmos is *ex nihilo* (from nothing) yet *through the Logos*
- Time itself begins with creation (no "before" creation in the temporal sense)

---

### **B. St. Athanasius - *Contra Gentes* §§40-44 and *De Incarnatione* §§1-5**

**The Logos as Creator and Sustainer:**

**Key Passage (*De Inc.* 3):**
"For God has made all things out of nothing through His own Word, our Lord Jesus Christ; and of all these His earthly creatures, He reserved especial pity for the race of men. Upon them, therefore, upon men who, as animals, were essentially impermanent, He bestowed a grace which other creatures lacked—namely the impress of His own Image, a share in the reality of the Word Himself."

**Athanasius's Argument Structure:**

1. **Creation Through the Logos (Johannine Theology):**
   - John 1:3: "All things were made through Him [the Logos], and without Him was not any thing made that was made."
   - The Logos is *not* a creature but the eternal Son, consubstantial with the Father
   - Creation is the act of the *Trinity*: Father wills, Son executes, Spirit perfects

2. **The Goodness of Creation:**
   - Genesis 1:31: "God saw everything that He had made, and behold, it was very good."
   - Against Gnostic dualism: Matter is *good*, created by the good God
   - Evil is not a substance but a *privation* (lack of good)

3. **The Image of God (v. 26-27):**
   - Humanity is created "in the image" (*kat' eikona*) of God
   - The Image is the Logos Himself; humans are *eikones* (icons) of the Image
   - This establishes human dignity and the possibility of *theosis* (deification)

**Theological Implications:**
- **Christology:** The Incarnation is a re-creation, recapitulating Adam
- **Soteriology:** Salvation restores the defaced Image
- **Anthropology:** Humans are *microcosms*, uniting spiritual and material creation

---

### **C. St. John Chrysostom - *Homilies on Genesis* 2-3**

**Chrysostom's Pastoral-Theological Method:**

Chrysostom interprets Genesis 1 with sensitivity to:
- The *oikonomia* (divine economy/pedagogy): God reveals progressively
- The anthropomorphic language: God speaks in human terms (*synkatabasis*)
- Moral application: Creation teaches virtue, gratitude, stewardship

**On "In the Beginning" (Homily 2.1):**
- "The beginning of time, the beginning of creation, the beginning of all things visible and invisible."
- Time is *created*; God is timeless
- Refutes Origen's speculation about *protological* souls preexisting creation

**On "God Said, Let There Be Light" (Homily 3.4):**
- "God's Word is deed. For Him to speak is to create."
- The divine *fiat* is immediate and effortless: God speaks, and it is
- Contrast with pagan myths: No struggle, no resistance, no preexisting chaos

**On the Goodness of Creation (Homily 3.6):**
- "Take note of God's love for humanity: He looks at all He has made and calls it 'very good,' preparing the world as a palace for the king about to enter—namely, man."
- Creation exists *for* humanity's benefit and salvation
- Ecological theology avant la lettre: The cosmos has intrinsic value as God's handiwork

---

**SECTION III: JEWISH INTERPRETATIONS - MIDRASH, RASHI, MAIMONIDES**

### **A. Rabbinic Midrash - *Bereshit Rabbah* 1:1**

**Text:**
"Rabbi Hoshaiah opened: 'I was by Him as a nursling (*amon*), a source of delight every day' (Proverbs 8:30). *Amon* means pedagogue; *amon* means covered; *amon* means hidden; some say *amon* means great... 'I was the working tool of the Holy One, blessed be He.'"

**Interpretation:**
- The Torah (Wisdom) preexisted creation and served as the *blueprint*
- God "looked into the Torah" and created the world accordingly
- Parallel Christian reading: The Logos-Wisdom is the eternal archetype

---

### **B. Rashi (Rabbi Shlomo Yitzhaki, 1040-1105) - Commentary on Genesis 1:1**

**Rashi's Opening Question:**
"The text should have begun with 'This month shall be unto you the first of months' (Exodus 12:2), which is the first commandment given to Israel. Why then does it begin with Creation?"

**Rashi's Answer:**
"To establish God's right to give the Land to Israel and to take it from the nations. If the nations say, 'You are robbers, for you conquered the lands of the seven nations,' Israel can reply, 'The whole earth belongs to the Holy One, blessed be He. He created it and gave it to whom He saw fit.'"

**Christian Response:**
- Rashi's reading is *political-national*
- Christian exegesis is *Christological-universal*: The land is *the cosmos*, and the inheritance is *eternal life* in the new creation (Romans 4:13)

---

### **C. Maimonides - *Guide for the Perplexed* II.13-30**

**Maimonides's Position:**
1. **Creation vs. Eternity:** The world is *created*, not eternal (contra Aristotle)
2. **Against Kalam:** But not through temporal causation (God is timeless)
3. **Limits of Reason:** Creation cannot be *demonstrated* by philosophy alone; it is accepted by faith

**Philosophical Argument:**
- *Aristotle's eternity of the world:* Unproven; Aristotle himself acknowledges uncertainty
- *Biblical testimony:* More reliable than Greek speculation
- *Maimonides's conclusion:* *Creatio ex nihilo* is philosophically possible and scripturally revealed

**Christian Appropriation:**
- Aquinas (*ST* I, q.46, a.2) agrees: Creation cannot be *demonstrated* but is *revealed* and *defensible*
- Both Maimonides and Aquinas synthesize Jerusalem and Athens

**SECTION IV: THE TRINITARIAN STRUCTURE OF CREATION**

### **A. The Father as *Archē* (Source)**

- **Biblical Foundation:** 1 Cor 8:6 - "One God, the Father, from whom (*ex hou*) are all things"
- The Father is the *fons divinitatis* (fountain of divinity)
- Creation proceeds *from* the Father *through* the Son *in* the Spirit

### **B. The Son as *Logos* (Mediator)**

- **Biblical Foundation:** John 1:3, Col 1:16-17, Heb 1:2
- All things were made *through* (*dia*) the Logos
- The Logos is the *exemplar cause*: creatures participate in the divine *logoi* (Maximus the Confessor)

**Maximus the Confessor - *Ambigua* 7:**
"God, who brought beings into existence from nothing, ... possesses the *logoi* of created beings... these *logoi* are the wills of God... For God brought beings into existence not because He needed them, but so that He might rejoice in His works (Ps 103:31)."

**Implication:** Creation is not arbitrary but reflects eternal divine Ideas in the Logos

### **C. The Holy Spirit as *Perfector***

- **Biblical Foundation:** Gen 1:2 - "The Spirit of God was hovering (*merachepet*) over the waters"
- LXX: ἐπεφέρετο (*epephereto*) - "was borne upon"
- Syriac: *mrahpa* - "brooding" (as a bird over eggs)

**St. Basil - *On the Holy Spirit* XVI.38:**
"In the creation, the Spirit is present as well, renewing the face of the earth... just as the power of the Father is present through the Son, so the power of the Spirit is present through the Son."

**Trinitarian Formula:**
- **Father:** Source (*aitia*)
- **Son:** Means (*dia*)
- **Spirit:** Perfection (*en*)

---

**SECTION V: CREATIO EX NIHILO - AGAINST ETERNAL MATTER**

### **The Platonic Challenge - *Timaeus* 27d-29a**

**Plato's Cosmogony:**
- The Demiurge (Craftsman) shapes preexisting matter (*chora*, the receptacle)
- Matter is *eternal*, though chaotic and formless
- The Demiurge imposes *Forms* upon matter, creating the cosmos

**Christian Rejection:**
1. **Ontological:** If matter is eternal, God is not the sole ultimate reality (dualism)
2. **Soteriological:** If matter is inherently chaotic/evil, salvation is escape *from* matter (Gnosticism)
3. **Scriptural:** Genesis 1:1 asserts absolute beginning: "In the beginning, God created..."

### **Patristic Arguments for *Ex Nihilo***

**Theophilus of Antioch - *To Autolycus* II.4 (c. 180 AD):**
"God, being His own place, containing all things, and alone uncreated, made the universe by His Word... For if matter were uncreated, God would not be the Creator of all, which is impious."

**Irenaeus - *Against Heresies* II.10.4:**
"It is proper to God, and God alone, to create and make whatsoever He wishes, while it is proper to His creatures that they be made... For the things that are made must have a beginning of their making; but God alone is uncreated."

**Athanasius - *Contra Gentes* 41:**
"God is good—or rather, the source of all goodness... And being good, He grudged existence to none. Therefore, He made all things out of nothing through His own Word."

**SECTION VI: THE LOGOS-WISDOM CHRISTOLOGY IN GENESIS 1**

### **The Identification of *Archē* with Christ**

**John 1:1-3 and Genesis 1:1 - The Johannine Synthesis:**
- *En archē* (In the beginning) directly echoes Genesis 1:1 LXX
- The Logos *was* (*ēn*, imperfect - eternal existence) in the beginning
- The Logos *was with God* (*pros ton theon* - distinct person)
- The Logos *was God* (*theos ēn* - divine nature)

**Patristic Consensus:**
- Christ *is* the *Archē* - not merely present *at* the beginning but *is* the Principle through whom creation occurs
- Revelation 3:14: "The Amen, the faithful and true witness, the *archē* of God's creation"
  - Arian misreading: Christ is the *first creature*
  - Orthodox reading: Christ is the *origin/source* of creation (not Himself created)

### **Proverbs 8:22-31 and the Wisdom Christology**

**Text (LXX):**
"The Lord *created* (*ektisen*) me as the *beginning* (*archēn*) of His ways for His works..."

**Arian Exploitation:**
- Arius: "*Ektisen* = created; therefore, Christ (Wisdom) is a creature"
- Council of Nicaea (325): This is a misreading

**Orthodox Response (Athanasius, *Contra Arianos* II.44-82):**
1. **Contextual Reading:** Proverbs 8 speaks of Wisdom's role in creation, not Wisdom's origin
2. **Incarnational Reading:** "*Ektisen* me" refers to the *Incarnation*, not the eternal generation
3. **"*Archēn* of His ways":** Christ is the *principle* of God's creative works, not a product of them

**Theological Synthesis:**
- Wisdom = Logos = Son of God
- Eternal, not created
- Through Him, all things were made (John 1:3)

**SECTION VII: CONTEMPORARY THEOLOGICAL IMPLICATIONS**

### **A. Science and Theology - The Big Bang and Genesis 1:1**

**The Scientific Consensus:**
- The universe began ~13.8 billion years ago in a singularity (Big Bang)
- Space, time, matter, and energy all came into existence at t=0
- Before the Big Bang, "before" is meaningless (time itself begins)

**Orthodox Theological Assessment:**

**Convergence with *Creatio Ex Nihilo*:**
- Modern cosmology affirms an absolute beginning (contra Greek eternity)
- Time is *created* alongside matter (Augustinian-Thomistic view vindicated)
- No "before" creation in temporal sense

**Divergence from Literalism:**
- The "six days" are not 24-hour periods but figurative/theological structuring
- St. Augustine (*De Genesi ad Litteram*): Days are logical, not temporal
- The Church Fathers (Origen, Gregory of Nyssa) already rejected literalism

**Theological Non-Negotiables:**
1. God created *freely* (not by necessity)
2. Creation is *good* (not evil or flawed)
3. Humans are created *in the image of God* (not mere animals)
4. Creation has *purpose* (not random chance)

**Modern Orthodox Voices:**
- **Fr. Georges Florovsky:** "Creation is the first act of divine revelation"
- **Fr. John Meyendorff:** "Science describes *how*; theology reveals *why* and *Who*"
- **Metropolitan Kallistos Ware:** "The Bible is not a science textbook; it is a theology book"

### **B. Ecological Theology - Stewardship of Creation**

**Genesis 1:26-28 - Dominion vs. Domination:**

**The Text:**
"Let us make man in our image, after our likeness. And let them have dominion (*radah*) over the fish of the sea and over the birds of the heavens..."

**Misinterpretation (Lynn White Jr., 1967):**
- Christianity's "dominion" theology caused ecological crisis
- Western exploitation of nature rooted in Genesis 1:28

**Orthodox Response:**
1. **Stewardship, Not Exploitation:** *Radah* (dominion) implies *royal responsibility*, not tyranny
2. **Priestly Role:** Humanity is the priest of creation, offering the cosmos back to God in thanksgiving (Eucharist)
3. **Cosmic Redemption:** Salvation includes all creation (Romans 8:19-22)

**Patristic Ecological Insight:**
- **St. Maximus the Confessor:** Humans are *microcosms*, uniting spiritual and material realms
- **St. John Chrysostom:** The earth is a common table; hoarding is theft against creation
- **St. Basil the Great:** Animals praise God in their own way; respect them

**Contemporary Application:**
- **Ecumenical Patriarch Bartholomew I:** "Ecological sin" is a category of sin
- **Orthodox Church Calendar:** September 1 = Day of Creation (environmental prayer)

**Theological Implication:**
- Creation is not *mute matter* but *living worship*

- Redemption restores creation to its liturgical purpose

#### **2. "Genesis 1:26-27 - The Imago Dei: Ontological Dignity, Hylemorphic Anthropology, and the Telos of Theosis"**

**ABSTRACT:** "Let us make man in our image, after our likeness" constitutes the charter of human dignity, the foundation of Christian anthropology, and the key to soteriology. Orthodox theology distinguishes *image* (*eikōn*, ontological) from *likeness* (*homoiōsis*, teleological): humanity *is* the image of God but *becomes* the likeness through theosis. This entry examines the Patristic consensus (Irenaeus, Athanasius, Gregory of Nyssa, John Damascene), Thomistic integration, contemporary bioethical implications, and the eschatological fulfillment of humanity in the Incarnate Logos.

---

**SECTION I: THE TEXT**

**Hebrew:**
וַיֹּאמֶר אֱלֹהִים נַעֲשֶׂה אָדָם בְּצַלְמֵנוּ כִּדְמוּתֵנוּ
*Vayomer Elohim na'aseh adam b'tzalmeinu kidmuteinu*

**Key Terms:**
- *Tzelem* (צֶלֶם) = image, likeness, representation
- *Demut* (דְּמוּת) = likeness, similarity, resemblance

**Greek (LXX):**
καὶ εἶπεν ὁ θεός Ποιήσωμεν ἄνθρωπον κατ' εἰκόνα ἡμετέραν καὶ καθ' ὁμοίωσιν
*Kai eipen ho theos Poiēsōmen anthrōpon kat' eikona hēmeteran kai kath' homoiōsin*

**Key Terms:**
- *Eikōn* (εἰκών) = image, icon, representation
- *Homoiōsis* (ὁμοίωσις) = likeness, similitude, becoming-like

**Critical Note:** Genesis 1:27 omits "likeness" in the fulfillment:
"So God created man in His own *image* (*b'tzalmo/kat' eikona*); in the image of God He created him..."

**Patristic Interpretation:** The *image* is given; the *likeness* is to be acquired.

---

**SECTION II: PATRISTIC EXEGESIS**

### **A. St. Irenaeus of Lyons - *Against Heresies* V.6.1, V.16.2**

**The Image-Likeness Distinction:**

**Text (*AH* V.6.1):**
"Now the image (*imago*) of God is man, and the likeness (*similitudo*) is the assimilation to God which is accomplished by perfection. For man received the honor of God's image (*imaginem*) at his first formation, but the likeness (*similitudinem*) he will receive afterwards at his consummation."

**Irenaeus's Anthropology:**
1. **Image** = the rational soul, free will, capacity for communion with God
2. **Likeness** = the *Holy Spirit* dwelling in the person, perfecting the image
3. **The Fall:** Adam lost the likeness (Spirit) but retained the image (rational nature)
4. **Redemption:** Christ restores the Spirit, enabling the recovery of likeness

**Soteriological Framework:**
- Creation → Fall → Incarnation → Theosis
- The image is *natural*; the likeness is *supernatural* (by grace)

---

### **B. St. Athanasius - *On the Incarnation* §3-5**

**The Defacement and Restoration of the Image:**

**Key Passage (*De Inc.* 5):**
"For God created man in His own image (*kat' eikona*), and made him after His own likeness (*kath' homoiōsin*), that he might continue in blessedness, having God as his possession and inheriting His eternity... But men, having turned from eternal things to things corruptible, through the counsel of the devil, became the cause of their own corruption in death."

**Athanasius's Doctrine:**
1. **The Image:** Participation in the Logos; rationality, immortality (originally)
2. **The Fall:** Image *defaced* (not destroyed), humans subject to death
3. **The Incarnation:** The Logos *assumes* human nature to *re-imprint* the image
4. **Theosis:** "He became man that we might become divine" (*De Inc.* 54)

**Christological Necessity:**
- Only the Image Himself (the Logos) can restore His own image in humanity
- The Incarnation is *re-creation* (recapitulation of Adam)

---

### **C. St. Gregory of Nyssa - *On the Making of Man* (De Opificio Hominis)**

**The Most Philosophical Patristic Anthropology:**

**Gregory's Key Theses:**

**1. The Composite Nature of Humanity (ch. 2-3):**
- Humanity is *microcosm* (*mikros kosmos*), uniting intellectual (soul) and material (body)
- Not a Platonic "soul imprisoned in body" but a *substantial union*
- The body is *good*, created by God, and destined for resurrection

**2. The Image of God (ch. 16-18):**
- **What the Image Is NOT:** Physical form (God is incorporeal)
- **What the Image IS:**
  - Rationality (*logikon*)
  - Freedom (*autexousion*)
  - Capacity for virtue and communion with God
  - Sovereignty over creation (reflecting God's sovereignty over all)

**3. Male and Female in the Image (ch. 16.9):**
**Text:** "There is in the Image no differentiation of male and female... for the divine is not divided into male and female."

**Gregory's Innovation:**
- Sexual differentiation is *addition* to the image (for reproduction after the Fall foreseen)
- In the resurrection, sexual distinction remains (identity) but procreative function ceases (Matt 22:30)
- Equality of sexes: both male and female equally bear the image

---

### **D. St. John of Damascus - *Exposition of the Orthodox Faith* II.12**

**Systematic Synthesis of Patristic Consensus:**

**The Image Defined:**
"The expression 'according to the image' (*kat' eikona*) indicates rationality and free will, while the expression 'according to the likeness' (*kath' homoiōsin*) indicates assimilation to God through virtue."

**The Threefold Image:**
1. **Natural Image:** Rationality, free will, immortality (ontological)
2. **Restored Image:** Baptism and sanctification (sacramental)
3. **Perfected Image:** Theosis, complete conformity to Christ (eschatological)

**Damascene's Hylemorphism:**
- Body and soul are *united substantially* (not accidentally)
- The soul is the *form* of the body (Aristotelian influence)
- At death, separation occurs, but resurrection reunites body and soul

---

**SECTION III: THE "LET US" - TRINITARIAN DISCLOSURE OR PLURALIS MAIESTATIS?**

### **Competing Interpretations:**

**1. Jewish Interpretation - Pluralis Maiestatis (Plural of Majesty):**
- God uses the royal "we" (like monarchs: "We decree...")
- No plurality in God's essence (strict monotheism)

**2. Jewish Interpretation - Angelic Council:**
- God consults with angels before creating humanity
- *Midrash Rabbah*: "God took counsel with the ministering angels"
- Refutation: Angels are *creatures*; they cannot participate in *creation*

**3. Christian Interpretation - Trinitarian Plurality:**
- "Let *us*" and "our image" indicates plurality of persons
- Confirmed by John 1:1-3, Col 1:16, Heb 1:2 (Son as co-creator)
- The plural is internal divine dialogue (Father, Son, Spirit)

**Patristic Consensus:**
- **Basil:** "*Us*" refers to the Son and Spirit; creation is Trinitarian act
- **Augustine (*De Trinitate* XII.6):** "To whom does God say '*Let us make*' except to the Son and the Holy Spirit?"

---

**SECTION IV: WHAT CONSTITUTES THE IMAGE?**

### **Survey of Patristic Positions:**

**1. Rationality (Logos-Likeness):**
- **Athanasius, Gregory of Nyssa, John Damascene:** The soul's rational capacity
- Humans can *know* God, contemplate truth, and reason about being
- This is the *Logos*-likeness (we are rational because God is the *Logos*)

**2. Free Will (Autexousion):**
- **Gregory of Nyssa, Maximus:** Freedom is constitutive of personhood
- God is absolutely free; humans possess derivative freedom
- Sin is the *misuse* of freedom, not its essence

**3. Immortality (Originally):**
- **Athanasius:** Humans were created for immortality, lost through sin
- Immortality is not natural (only God is immortal by nature) but *gifted*

**4. Dominion Over Creation:**
- **Chrysostom:** The image includes *stewardship* (Genesis 1:26-28)
- Humans are *priests* of creation, mediating between God and cosmos

**5. Capacity for Theosis:**
- **Maximus the Confessor:** The image is the potential for *becoming* divine by grace
- We bear the image so we can *participate* in the divine life

---

**SECTION V: THOMISTIC-SCHOLASTIC DEVELOPMENT**

### **St. Thomas Aquinas - *Summa Theologiae* I, q.93**

**Aquinas's Systematic Treatment:**

**Article 1: Whether the Image of God is in Man?**
- **Response:** Yes. The image consists in the intellectual nature (mind, intellect, will).

**Article 2: Whether the Image of God is in Irrational Creatures?**
- **Response:** No. Only *traces* (*vestigia*) of God are in irrational creatures.
  - **Trace:** Effect resembling cause remotely (e.g., footprint)
  - **Image:** Effect resembling cause proximately (e.g., reflection in mirror)

**Article 4: Whether the Image of God is Found in Every Man?**
- **Response:** Yes, but in different degrees:
  - **Image by Nature:** Rationality (all humans)
  - **Image by Conformity of Grace:** Sanctification (the redeemed)
  - **Image by Likeness of Glory:** Beatific Vision (the saints in heaven)

**Article 6: Whether the Image of God is in Man as Regards the Mind Only?**
- **Response:** Primarily yes (the mind), but secondarily the whole person (body included).
- The body is *indirectly* in the image because it is the *instrument* of the rational soul.

**SECTION VI: CONTEMPORARY BIOETHICAL IMPLICATIONS**

### **A. Abortion and the Imago Dei**

**Theological Argument:**
- If humans are created *in the image of God*, then human life has intrinsic, infinite worth
- This worth is *ontological*, not functional (i.e., not dependent on abilities)
- The unborn child is *fully human* from conception (body + rational soul = person)

**Patristic Testimony:**
- **Didache 2:2 (c. 100 AD):** "You shall not murder a child by abortion."
- **Tertullian (*Apology* 9.8):** "It is murder to prevent a birth, and it makes no difference whether one destroys a life already born or does away with it in its nascent stage."
- **Basil the Great (*Epistle* 188.2):** "A woman who deliberately destroys a fetus is guilty of murder."

**Orthodox Position:**
- Human personhood begins at conception
- Abortion is the taking of innocent human life (murder)
- Exceptions: Only when the mother's life is in imminent danger (principle of double effect)

---

### **B. Euthanasia and the Sanctity of Life**

**Theological Argument:**
- Life is a *gift* from God, not a *possession* we own
- Humans are *stewards*, not *owners*, of their lives
- Suicide and euthanasia violate the image of God in the person

**Patristic Witness:**
- **Augustine (*City of God* I.20):** "It is never lawful to kill oneself... for if no one has the right to kill even a guilty person... much less has he the right to kill himself."

**Contemporary Application:**
- Rejection of "death with dignity" arguments (dignity is *intrinsic*, not dependent on quality of life)
- Palliative care is supported; direct killing is condemned

---

### **C. Transhumanism and the Enhancement of Humanity**

**The Challenge:**
- Transhumanists seek to *transcend* biological limitations via technology
- Goal: Enhanced intelligence, longevity, physical capabilities

**Theological Concerns:**
1. **Instrumentalization:** Treating the body as a *machine* to be upgraded (reductionism)
2. **Replacement vs. Restoration:** Therapy (restoring function) is permissible; enhancement (surpassing nature) risks *hubris*
3. **Inequality:** Enhancement creates a genetic underclass

**Orthodox Response:**
- **Body as Temple:** The body is sacred, the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Cor 6:19)
- **Theosis vs. Transhumanism:** The goal is *spiritual* transformation, not *technological* augmentation
- The resurrection body is the ultimate "enhancement" - incorruptible, glorious, powerful (1 Cor 15:42-44)

---

**SECTION VII: THE ESCHATOLOGICAL FULFILLMENT OF THE IMAGE**

### **The Restoration of All Things (Apokatastasis Pantōn)**

**Biblical Foundation:**
- Acts 3:21: "Whom heaven must receive until the time of *apokatastasis pantōn*"
- Colossians 1:20: "Through Him to reconcile to Himself all things"

**Maximus the Confessor - The Cosmic Christ:**
- Christ is the *recapitulation* (*anakephalaiōsis*) of all creation
- In Him, the image of God is *fully* manifested (Col 1:15: "image of the invisible God")
- Redemption is *re-creation*: restoring the defaced image to its original glory

**The Resurrection Body:**
- 1 Corinthians 15:49: "As we have borne the image of the earthly [Adam], we shall also bear the image of the heavenly [Christ]"
- Philippians 3:21: "He will transform our lowly body to be like His glorious body"
- The image-likeness reaches *perfection* in the resurrection

---

**CONCLUSION:**

The *imago Dei* is not a static possession but a *dynamic vocation*: humans *are* the image ontologically and *become* the likeness eschatologically. This doctrine grounds human dignity, animates the pursuit of virtue (sanctification), and finds its fulfillment in the resurrection, when the image is fully conformed to the Image Himself, Jesus Christ.
